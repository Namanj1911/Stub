Privacy policy

Everything stays on your phone. We collect nothing.

Stub has no account, no sign-in, and no server. Your log, goal, settings, and progress live only in the app's local storage on your device. The app sends none of your data anywhere — no analytics, no advertising, no tracking, no third parties. On the App Store this is declared as Data Not Collected.

  • Export — you can export your data as a JSON file from the Profile screen. It goes only where you choose to share it.
  • Notifications — reminders and milestones are scheduled locally on your device. No push servers are involved.
  • Backups — your iPhone's own backup (iCloud or computer) may include Stub's data, like any app's. That is Apple's system, governed by your iOS settings and Apple's privacy policy.

Deleting your data

Because nothing leaves your phone, deletion is entirely in your hands: the reset option in Stub's Profile screen erases everything the app holds, and so does deleting the app itself. There is no copy anywhere else, so there is nothing further to request.

If this ever changes

If a future version of Stub ever sends anything off your device — for example, anonymous crash reporting — this policy and the App Store privacy label will be updated first, and any such feature will be described here before it ships.

A note on health information

Stub is intended for adult smokers who want to cut down. The health timeline, nicotine figures, and money estimates it shows are general information, not medical guidance. For advice about quitting smoking, talk to a doctor or a licensed cessation service.
